Special Items
Add some fun or action triggers using Items
Introduction to Special Items
Officially called "Items". But to avoid confusion with normal items, they are referred to as "Special Items" in this document.
It can be accessed through Housing Menu (Ghast Tear/Nether Star) > Items (Emerald).
List of Special Items
Mailbox
Players with "Item: Mailbox" permission can place down a Mailbox and the owner can right click it to view mails.
The players can right click a Mailbox to obtain a Book and Quill to submit a mail.
(Please note that all signed Books and Quills will go into the Mailbox)
Egg Hunt Block
Players with "Item: Egg Hunt" permission can place down Egg Hunt Blocks (hereafter referred as Eggs).
Right clicking one will start an Egg Hunt (repeatable) to locate all the eggs within the House.
There can be at max 25 Eggs in an Egg Hunt. Finding all placed Eggs will end the Egg Hunt and announce the time taken to finish it.
Teleport Pad
As the name suggests, this item teleports you from one Teleport Pad to the other. Players with "Item: Teleport Pad" permission can place and edit Teleport Pads.
You may choose the "color" of a Teleport Pad, they act as tags to identify them among one another.
Options include: Red, Yellow, Lime, Blue, Magenta, Orange, Gray, White, Green, Aqua, Purple, Black
You may set the destination of a certain Teleport Pad to another with the certain color, "Another House" or "Housing Lobby".
Launch Pad
Players with "Item: Launch Pad" permission can place down a Launch Pad and right click it to change its settings.
Toggle Mode: Toggles whether stepping on the Launch Pad launches the player "Forward" (4 directions) or "Facing" (all directions)
Change Launch Strength: Set the horizontal launching strength of the Launch Pad (value 1~12, default 5)
Change Vertical Strength: Set the vertical launching strength of the Launch Pad (value 1~12, default 5)
Parkour Block
Taking the appearance of the Light Weighted Pressure Plate, the owner can place a Parkour Block that creates a "Parkour Start", "Checkpoint #n" or "Parkour End" of a parkour.
The Parkour Block placed most recently is always the Parkour End. Whenever you place a new one, the previous Parkour End is turned into a Checkpoint.
There can be at max 20 Checkpoints (Not including Start and End) in a House.
(Note: If players that aren't the owner try to place down a Parkour Block, it becomes an ordinary pressure plate)
Action Pad
Players with "Item: Action Pad" permission can place and right click to edit an Action Pad's behaviour.
On default, it takes the appearance of a Heavy Weighted Pressure Plate, but it can also be customized to appear as Light Weighted Pressure Plate, Stone Pressure Plate, or Wooden Pressure Plate.
Hologram
Players with "Item: Hologram" permission can place and right click to edit a Hologram's content.
It displays text using Name Tags on invisible Armor Stands, and Formatting Codes can be used on a Hologram's text.
It can display at max 5 separate lines.
NPC
Players with "Item: NPCs" permission can place and right click while sneaking to edit an NPC.
It is extremely customizable, with its actions when clicked, name, appearance and equipped gear (including held item) being able to be edited.
You may also toggle whether its name tag is hidden, and whether it looks at nearby players.
Action Button
Players with "Item: Action Button" permission can place and right click while sneaking to edit an Action Button's behaviour.
On default, it takes the appearance of a Stone Button, but it can also be customized to appear as a Wooden Button. An Action Button behaves exactly the same as an Action Pad, except it is instead in the form of a Button.
Leaderboard
Players with "Item: Leaderboard" permission can place and right click to edit a Leaderboard's settings.
It displays the players (and the stat value) that has the highest (or lowest) stat in the House.
Below are its available settings:
Data Provider: Toggles what the Leaderboard displays (Parkour Leaderboard / Player Variable / Team Variable)
Title Override: Customize what is displayed at the top of the Leaderboard, replacing the default
Line Count: Set the amount of players shown on the Leaderboard
Variable: Set the stat displayed on the Leaderboard
Format Type: Set how the numbers are displayed (Number [no commas] / Number (Formatted) [with commas] / Roman Numerals / Duration - Minutes, Seconds / Duration - Days, Hours, Minutes, Seconds)
Invert Sorting: Toggle whether the players with the highest or lowest stat are on top
Show All Entries: Toggle whether players with stat equal to or lower than zero are displayed
Persistent Data: Toggle whether offline players are displayed
Show Personal: Toggle whether the viewing player's stat is displayed at the bottom if they are not within the Leaderboard
Player Conditions: Add filters to which players are displayed
Match Any Conditions: Toggle whether players only have to meet at least 1 Condition to be displayed
Trash Can
Players with "Item: Trash Can" permission can place a Trash Can.
Right clicking a Trash Can opens an inventory with 27 item slots.
Any item thrown into the Trash Can's inventory is deleted after closing the UI (this action is NOT reversible).
Biome Stick
Players with "Item: Biome Stick" permission can use a Biome Stick.
It changes the biome within a custom radius (default 3) to a custom biome (default desert).
Available biomes include: Desert, Forest, Ice Plains, Jungle, Mesa, Ocean, Plains, Swamp, Savanna, Nether, Taiga, Birch Forest, Deep Ocean, Roofed Forest, End, Mushroom Island
Social Blocks
The owner can place down Social Blocks that links to their (or someone else's) social media.
There are currently 5 different social blocks, which are: Twitter, YouTube, Instagram, Twitch, Discord (Server or User)
